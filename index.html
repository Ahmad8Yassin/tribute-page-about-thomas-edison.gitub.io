Thomas Edison

Revolutionizing Recorded Sound Technology.

Thomas Edison invented the general electric light bulb which changed the world.

Here's a time line of Thomas Edison life:

  • 1847 - Thomas Edison was born in Ohio, United States.
  • 1862 - Edison began his work in the telegraph and electrical supply industry.
  • 1877 - Edison invented the phonograph, which allowed for recording and playback of recorded sound.
  • 1879 - Edison invented the electric light bulb that relied on electricity for power.
  • 1882 - Edison established a trial electric power station in New York City.
  • 1887 - Edison invented the rotary cylinder phonograph, which allowed for continuous playback of recordings.
  • 1891 - Edison founded General Electric Company.
  • 1896 - Edison invented the first motion picture system that worked with a vertically-moving film.
  • 1914 - Edison successfully invented a device for detecting submarines using sound waves.
  • 1931 - Thomas Edison passed away at the age of 84 in New Jersey.
